D2Attitude 駕駛態度

Sources: The Highway Code Rules 144–150, 160–162, 264 · Road Traffic Act 1988 · DfT National Statistics: Contributory Factors in Reported Road Casualties · Think! road safety campaign

Your attitude directly affects how you drive and how you interact with other road users. Poor attitude — aggression, impatience, selfishness — is a contributory factor in a significant proportion of all road accidents.

態度係考試常考題目。重點係：禮讓、耐性、唔好衝動反應。記住：冇人有絕對優先權。

Consideration & Courtesy 體諒與禮貌

  • Nobody has an absolute right of way — be prepared to give way even when you technically have priority
  • Thank drivers who give way to you (a wave or nod)
  • Be patient with: learner drivers (L-plates), older drivers, cyclists, pedestrians, horse riders
  • Allow adequate space and time for vulnerable road users
  • Plan journeys so you're not rushed — most dangerous attitude-related driving stems from being late
唔好因為趕時間而衝動。計劃行程要預留充裕時間，避免「鬥快」心態。

Aggressive Driving & Road Rage 攻擊性駕駛與路怒

⚠ Road rage is a criminal offence
Aggressive driving behaviour can result in prosecution for careless/dangerous driving, public order offences, or assault.
  • Don't react to aggressive drivers — avoid eye contact, don't retaliate
  • If followed by an aggressive driver: drive to a busy public place or police station
  • Don't use the horn aggressively — horn is only a warning of your presence, not a tool to vent frustration
  • Tailgating (following too closely) is dangerous and can result in prosecution for careless driving
  • Flashing headlights: only to warn of your presence — NOT to demand that someone moves, NOT to indicate it's safe for someone to proceed
  • If you are the target of road rage: stay calm, don't escalate; report to police if you feel threatened (999 if immediate danger, 101 otherwise)
有人閃燈係警示你佢嘅存在，唔係叫你去行。唔好用喇叭發洩不滿。

Lane Discipline 行車線紀律

  • Keep left at all times unless overtaking — this is the law, not just a suggestion
  • Middle lane hogging: staying in the middle lane when the left lane is clear is an offence (Highway Code Rule 264) — fixed penalty £100 + 3 penalty points
  • Don't weave between lanes — maintain a consistent, predictable lane position
  • Always signal before changing lanes
  • Don't "undertake" (overtake on the left) except in slow-moving queues or when the vehicle ahead is turning right
中線滯留係犯法！£100罰款加3分。超車後應盡快返左線。

Priority at Junctions & Roundabouts 路口優先權

  • When emerging from a side road, give way to traffic already on the main road
  • When turning into a road, give way to pedestrians already crossing
  • At a roundabout: give way to traffic from the right (already on the roundabout)
  • Give way to buses pulling out from bus stops when it is safe to do so
  • Emergency vehicles: pull over safely to let them pass — do not mount the pavement, do not run a red light to clear the way
讓急救車通過時，唔好闖紅燈！停喺安全位置讓佢過。

Responsible Driving 負責任駕駛

  • Anticipation: think ahead, read the road early — smooth driving is safer and more efficient
  • Never drive under peer pressure — no dare, race, or "just this once" is worth a death or serious injury
  • Don't drive when emotionally stressed — anger, grief, or extreme anxiety impair concentration and reaction time
  • Horn restrictions: do not use your horn in a built-up area between 23:30 and 07:00 (unless an emergency)
  • Show patience on narrow roads — one vehicle may need to reverse to a passing place
  • Driving with courtesy reduces accidents — the Highway Code emphasises cooperation between all road users
晚上11:30至早上7:00喺市區唔可以用喇叭（緊急情況除外）。任何情況下都唔好因同伴壓力而冒險。

Attitude Towards Vulnerable Users 對弱勢使用者嘅態度

  • Slow down and give extra space near schools, playgrounds, bus stops
  • Be especially careful near parked ice cream vans — children may run out unpredictably
  • Cyclists: give at least 1.5 metres when overtaking; they are allowed to ride in the centre of the lane (to avoid drains, parked car doors etc.)
  • Horses: pass wide and slow (10mph or less); don't sound horn; don't rev engine
  • If a driver is elderly or confused, be patient — they may be slower to react
