D5Hazard Awareness 危險意識

Sources: The Highway Code Rules 159–169 · DVSA Hazard Perception Test guidance · The Official DVSA Guide to Driving – the essential skills (8th edition)

Anticipating hazards is the single most important skill for safe driving and a major part of the test.

預見危險係安全駕駛最重要技能，亦係考試重點（特別係Hazard Perception Test）。

Routines & Acronyms 駕駛口訣

MSM — Mirror, Signal, Manoeuvre

Used before every change of speed or direction.

  • Mirror — check mirrors to assess what's around
  • Signal — if necessary, in good time
  • Manoeuvre — carry out the action safely

PSL — Position, Speed, Look (at junctions)

  • Position correctly in lane
  • Speed — adjust appropriately
  • Look — into the new road for traffic

IPSGA — The "System of Car Control" (advanced)

  • Information (take + give)
  • Position
  • Speed
  • Gear
  • Acceleration

Types of Hazards 危險類型

  • Static / physical hazards: junctions, bends, hills, road narrowing, parked cars
  • Moving hazards: other vehicles, pedestrians, cyclists, animals
  • Environmental hazards: weather, light conditions, road surface
  • Driver-related hazards: tiredness, distraction, emotional state

Pass Mark
  • 14 clips of about 1 minute each, filmed from a car's perspective
  • One clip contains 2 developing hazards (so 15 hazards total)
  • Click the mouse when you see a developing hazard — one that requires you to take action (change speed/direction)
  • Score up to 5 points per hazard — earlier the click, higher the score
  • Click continuously or in a pattern = 0 for that clip (system detects cheating)
  • You only get ONE attempt per clip — no review
14段一分鐘片段（共15個危險），愈早click分愈高（最高5分）。千祈不可亂click或連續click，系統會偵測作弊，該段0分。
💡 Strategy

Aim for 2–3 clicks per hazard: one as soon as you suspect it's developing, another to confirm, maybe a third. Don't over-click — be deliberate.

Distractions to Avoid 避免分心

  • Using a mobile phone (handheld = illegal, 6 points + £200 fine)
  • Eating, drinking, smoking
  • Loud music
  • Arguing with passengers
  • Adjusting sat nav while moving
  • Looking at maps
