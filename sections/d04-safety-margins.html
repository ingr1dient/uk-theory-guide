D4Safety Margins & Weather 安全距離與天氣

Sources: The Highway Code Rules 226–237 (adverse weather conditions) · Rule 126 (stopping distances in adverse conditions) · DVSA Safe Driving for Life

Wet Roads 濕滑路面

  • Stopping distance doubles (×2)
  • Use 4-second rule (instead of 2)
  • Brake gently and earlier
  • Watch for spray from large vehicles
  • Reduce speed in heavy rain — windscreen wipers may not cope

Aquaplaning 水滑現象

When tyres lose contact with the road surface, riding on a layer of water:

  • Symptoms: steering feels light; engine revs rise (wheels spin freely)
  • What to do: ease off the accelerator gradually; do NOT brake harshly; do NOT steer sharply
  • Wait until you feel the tyres regain grip, then continue normally
  • Prevention: proper tyre tread, lower speeds in wet
水滑：軚盤變輕、引擎轉速升高 = 輪胎離地。慢慢鬆油門，唔好急煞唔好急轉。等抓地力回復先繼續。

Ice & Snow 冰雪

  • Stopping distance can be ×10 (ten times!)
  • Drive in the highest gear possible — reduces wheelspin
  • Brake very gently, well in advance
  • Steering feels light = ice present
  • Use main roads where possible (more likely gritted)
  • Black ice: invisible, looks like wet road; common at dawn, dusk, bridges, shaded spots
  • Before setting off: clear all snow from car, roof, lights, windows, number plates
  • Driving with snow on roof / obscured plate / windows = fine
冰路停車距離增加十倍！用高檔位低轉速慢駛，輕踩煞車。小心「黑冰」 — 表面像普通濕路但其實非常滑。

Fog 霧

  • Slow down — you can't react to what you can't see
  • Use dipped headlights (full beam reflects back, reduces visibility further)
  • Switch on fog lights when visibility drops below 100m
  • Turn fog lights OFF when fog clears (dazzles other drivers, masks brake lights)
  • Keep extra distance — don't follow tail lights of vehicle ahead too closely
  • Listen — open window briefly, especially at junctions
  • Be especially careful at junctions

Sun Glare 陽光眩目

  • Use the sun visor
  • Slow down
  • Keep windscreen clean — smears worsen glare
  • Carry sunglasses
  • Especially bad: dawn (east), dusk (west), low winter sun

Wind 大風

  • Most affects: high-sided vehicles, caravans, motorcycles, cyclists
  • Be prepared for sudden gusts on exposed sections (bridges, gaps between hedges)
  • Give extra space to vulnerable users — they may swerve
  • Hold steering wheel firmly

Night Driving 夜間駕駛

  • Reduce speed — you can only see as far as your lights illuminate
  • Use full beam on dark unlit roads where safe
  • Dip to dipped beam when:
    • Meeting oncoming vehicles
    • Following another vehicle (don't dazzle the mirror)
    • In built-up areas with streetlights
  • If dazzled: slow down or stop; do not retaliate by flashing back
  • Look for pedestrians, animals, cyclists without lights

Flooded Roads 水浸路段

  • Avoid if possible — water depth is hard to judge
  • If you must drive through: in 1st gear, slow, steady revs, keep clutch slipping to keep revs up
  • Test brakes immediately after (drag lightly to dry)
  • If water at door height: turn back — risk of stalling and being swept away
