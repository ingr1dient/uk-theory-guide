Test Structure 試卷結構

The UK theory test has two parts taken at the same sitting:

Part 1 — Multiple Choice

50
Questions
57
Minutes
43
Pass Mark
86%
Required
  • 50 multiple-choice questions, mostly 1 answer correct (some require multiple)
  • One question is based on a short scenario / case study (e.g. driving in fog)
  • Flag uncertain questions — review at the end
  • Read carefully — the wrong answers are designed to look plausible

Part 2 — Hazard Perception

14
Video Clips
15
Hazards
75
Max Score
44
Pass Mark
  • 14 video clips, ~1 minute each, filmed from driver's perspective
  • One clip contains 2 developing hazards (the rest have 1)
  • Click when you see a developing hazard — earlier = higher score (max 5 per hazard)
  • Clicking continuously or in patterns = 0 for that clip (cheat detection)
  • You cannot review or change answers
⚠ Both Parts Must Pass Same Sitting
If you fail either part, you fail the whole test and must rebook. You'll need to wait at least 3 working days before retaking.

Booking the Test 預約考試

  • Book via the official DVSA website (gov.uk/book-theory-test)
  • Fee: £23 (as of recent years; check current price)
  • Bring valid UK photocard provisional driving licence — required for entry
  • If you have an old paper licence, also bring valid passport
  • Arrive at least 15 minutes early
  • No phones, notes, watches in the test room

After Passing 合格之後

  • You receive a theory test pass certificate
  • Valid for 2 years
  • You must pass your practical test within 2 years — otherwise the theory expires and you must retake it
  • Bring the certificate number to your practical test

If You Fail 若不合格

  • Wait minimum 3 working days before retaking
  • Pay the fee again
  • You'll receive feedback indicating which question category you struggled in
  • Focus revision on weak areas

Final Study Tips 最後溫習建議

  • Practice with multiple sources — official DVSA app, this guide, theory test pro websites
  • Take full mock tests under timed conditions
  • For Hazard Perception: practise with online clips — train your timing
  • Read the Highway Code at least once cover-to-cover
  • Read Know Your Traffic Signs publication (free PDF on gov.uk)
  • Don't memorise answers — understand WHY each answer is correct
  • Get a full night's sleep before the test
  • Eat before the test, but not too heavy
  • Stay calm — read each question twice before answering
✓ Good Luck! · 祝你考試順利！

You've got this. Steady preparation, understanding (not memorising), and calm test-taking will get you through. Once you pass, your practical lessons will reinforce the theory — they go hand in hand.
