⚠ The Safest Limit Is ZERO
Even small amounts of alcohol affect judgment, reaction time, and coordination. The only safe amount before driving is none.
酒後駕駛最安全的酒量係零。任何份量都影響反應同判斷。

Alcohol Units 酒精單位

1 unit = 10ml or 8g of pure alcohol. Approximate guide:

  • Pint of beer (4–5%): ~2 units
  • Pint of strong lager (5–6%): ~3 units
  • Standard glass of wine (175ml, 12%): ~2 units
  • Single spirit measure (25ml, 40%): 1 unit
  • Alcopop / bottle (275ml, 5%): ~1.5 units

How Alcohol Affects Driving 酒精對駕駛的影響

  • Slows reaction time
  • Impairs judgment of speed and distance
  • Reduces coordination
  • Creates false sense of confidence — you feel better than you are
  • Affects vision (blurred, double, narrowed field)
  • Reduces concentration

How Long Until Alcohol Clears? 幾耐先散得晒？

The body processes roughly 1 unit per hour, but this varies by individual, weight, sex, food eaten, health. There is NO way to speed this up:

  • Coffee does NOT sober you up — only makes you a wide-awake drunk
  • Cold shower does NOT help
  • Eating, drinking water — does NOT clear alcohol

Only TIME works.

身體大約每小時代謝一個unit，但因人而異。咖啡、水、食嘢、洗冷水澡都唔會加速。只有時間先可以散酒。
⚠ The "Morning After"
Heavy drinking the night before may leave you still over the limit the next morning. Many drink-driving convictions occur 8–12 hours after the last drink. 前一晚飲多咗，第二朝可能仲超標！

Drug Driving 藥物駕駛

  • Driving under the influence of illegal drugs = criminal offence, same penalties as drink driving
  • Police use roadside drug testing kits (saliva swabs) — test for cannabis, cocaine, etc.
  • Specific limits for prescription drugs (e.g., morphine, diazepam) — over limits = offence even if prescribed

Prescription & Over-the-Counter Medicines 處方及成藥

  • Some legal medicines impair driving — check warning labels ("may cause drowsiness")
  • Ask doctor / pharmacist if a medicine affects driving ability
  • If prescribed strong painkillers (opioids), antihistamines, sleeping pills — do NOT drive
  • Defence available if taken as prescribed AND not impaired — but burden on driver

Tiredness 疲勞駕駛

Driver fatigue causes 1 in 5 fatal road crashes. Tiredness affects you similarly to alcohol:

  • Take breaks every 2 hours on long journeys (at least 15 minutes)
  • Plan rest stops in advance
  • Don't start a long drive after a full working day
  • Warning signs: heavy eyelids, drifting in lane, missing exits, yawning, slow reactions
  • Best cure for sleep: SLEEP. A 15-min nap + caffeine helps short-term — but only sleep restores you fully
  • Open windows, music, food: do not reliably stop you falling asleep
  • Most dangerous times: 2am–6am and 2pm–4pm (natural body lows)
疲勞每5宗致命車禍就有1宗。最佳「解藥」係瞓覺。每兩小時休息15分鐘。

Carbon Monoxide 一氧化碳

  • Colourless, odourless gas from faulty exhausts
  • Causes drowsiness, headache, dizziness — can be fatal
  • Have exhaust system checked regularly
  • If you feel unwell — open windows, get fresh air, have car checked
