D6Vulnerable Road Users 弱勢道路使用者

Sources: The Highway Code Rules 195–225 · Highway Code Hierarchy of Road Users (2022 update) · Highway Code Rules 18–30 (crossings)

The Highway Code gives a clear hierarchy of road users — those most at risk are given the highest priority. Drivers of cars/vans have the greatest responsibility to reduce risk to others.

新版Highway Code明確道路使用者優先次序，弱勢者（行人、單車、電單車、騎馬人）優先。駕駛汽車要負最大責任保護其他人。
📋 Hierarchy of Road Users (most → least vulnerable)
Pedestrians → Cyclists → Horse riders → Motorcyclists → Cars/vans → Large vehicles

Pedestrians 行人

Crossing Types

CrossingHow it works
ZebraBlack/white stripes + flashing yellow Belisha beacons. Give way to anyone waiting.
PelicanPush-button signal-controlled. Flashing amber = give way to pedestrians on crossing, then proceed.
PuffinSensors detect pedestrians. No flashing amber phase. Red until clear.
Toucan"Two can" — pedestrians AND cyclists cross together. Wider than usual.
PegasusHorse-and-rider crossing. Higher button for riders, fenced approaches.
五種斑馬線：Zebra（黑白條）→ Pelican（黃燈閃可讓行）→ Puffin（感應式無閃黃）→ Toucan（人車共用）→ Pegasus（馬匹專用）。

Pedestrians Needing Extra Care

  • Blind/partially sighted: white cane, guide dog (yellow or white harness), or both
  • Deaf-blind: white cane with red bands
  • Elderly: may walk slowly, have difficulty judging speed
  • Children: unpredictable; especially near schools, parks, ice cream vans
  • Disabled: wheelchair users, walking frame, etc.
⚠ Highway Code Rule H2
Pedestrians waiting to cross at a junction have priority — drivers turning into or out of a side road MUST give way.

Cyclists 單車

  • Give cyclists at least 1.5 metres (5 feet) when overtaking at speeds up to 30 mph; more at higher speeds
  • Advanced Stop Lines (ASL) at traffic lights: cars must stop at the first white line; the box ahead is for cyclists only
  • Cyclists may ride two abreast — legal and often safer
  • Check mirrors and blind spot for cyclists before turning, opening doors, pulling away
  • Dutch reach: open driver's door with your left hand — forces you to look behind
超單車要留1.5米闊度。停燈時車要停喺第一條白線後（ASL專為單車預留位置）。落車時用左手開門 — 自然會回頭睇後方。

Motorcyclists 電單車

  • Often hard to see — small, narrow profile
  • Most vulnerable at junctions — drivers commonly fail to spot them ("SMIDSY" — Sorry, Mate, I Didn't See You)
  • Affected by wind, road surface, debris — may swerve
  • Use the "lifesaver" — over-the-shoulder check before manoeuvres

Horse Riders 騎馬者

  • Pass wide (at least 2m) and slow (max 10 mph)
  • Do not sound the horn or rev the engine — startles the horse
  • Be patient at narrow roads — horses can be unpredictable
  • Look for arm signals from riders
遇馬：闊2米、慢過10mph、唔好按喇叭、唔好踩油。

Older People 長者

  • May walk slowly, have impaired vision/hearing
  • Reactions slower — give extra time
  • May be confused at busy junctions

Children 兒童

  • Unpredictable — may run into road without looking
  • School crossing patrols ("lollipop people") — STOP when sign is raised, illegal to ignore
  • Extra care near schools, parks, residential streets, and ice cream vans
  • Watch for school buses with flashing lights

Pedestrians at Night 夜間行人

  • Much harder to see — especially if wearing dark clothes
  • Watch for reflective bands, light-coloured clothing
  • Use full beam where possible, dip when oncoming traffic or following close
