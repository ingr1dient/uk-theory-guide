D14Vehicle Loading 車輛負載

Sources: The Highway Code Rules 98–99 · Road Vehicles (Construction and Use) Regulations 1986 regs. 97–100 · DfT Loads on Roads guidance

Effects of Loading 負載的影響

  • Increases braking distance (more momentum to stop)
  • Affects handling — heavier vehicle more sluggish to steer
  • Affects fuel consumption — extra weight = more fuel
  • Loads must be secure — sudden braking can throw loose objects forward (including into people)
  • Roof loads affect stability — especially in crosswinds
  • Trailer / caravan: increases length, reduces visibility behind

Driver's Legal Responsibility 司機的法律責任

⚠ Driver Is Responsible
It is the driver's legal responsibility to ensure the vehicle is not overloaded and the load is properly secured — even if someone else loaded it. 車主／司機要負責確保車輛未超重，貨物已固定，唔可以推卸畀人。

Roof Racks 車頂架

  • Secure properly — tie down or use straps
  • Distribute weight evenly
  • Removes when not in use — reduces drag and fuel use
  • Check weight limit of rack AND vehicle's max roof load

Trailers & Caravans 拖車及露營車

  • Maximum speed when towing: 50 mph single carriageway, 60 mph dual carriageway / motorway (cars)
  • Trailer must NOT be wider than 2.55m
  • If trailer wider than the towing vehicle: extension mirrors required
  • Drivers who passed test before 1 Jan 1997 can tow up to 8,250kg (Cat B+E)
  • Drivers who passed after 1 Jan 1997 can tow trailers up to 3,500kg combined (since Dec 2021 — previously needed B+E test for over 750kg)
  • "Snaking" (side-to-side movement of trailer): ease off gently, don't brake hard, don't accelerate

Carrying Passengers 載客

  • All must wear seatbelts (legal)
  • Driver responsible for under-14s
  • Don't exceed manufacturer's stated passenger capacity
  • Children require appropriate child seats based on age/weight/height

Overloading 超載

  • Illegal — affects braking, steering, tyres, suspension
  • Penalty: fine, possible prohibition, points if very serious
  • Look up your vehicle's max gross weight in the V5C or owner's manual
