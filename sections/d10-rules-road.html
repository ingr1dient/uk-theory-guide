D10Rules of the Road 道路守則

Sources: The Highway Code Rules 130–163 (general driving rules) · Rules 184–190 (roundabouts) · Rules 293–294 (level crossings) · Rules 175–183 (junctions)

Roundabouts 迴旋處

  • Give way to traffic from the RIGHT (already on the roundabout)
  • Approach in the correct lane:
    • Turning left (first exit): left lane, signal LEFT on approach, keep signal until exit
    • Straight ahead (second exit): left lane usually, no signal on approach, signal LEFT after passing the exit before yours
    • Turning right (later exit) or full circle: right lane, signal RIGHT on approach, change to signal LEFT after passing exit before yours
  • Mini-roundabouts: same rules, but you must drive over the central white circle if possible (not a kerb)
  • Watch for cyclists and motorcyclists — they may stay in the left lane even when going right
迴旋處規則：讓右邊先行。第一出口 → 左燈左線；第二出口（直行）→ 過咗前一個出口先打左燈；右轉/兜圈 → 右燈右線，過咗前一個出口轉打左燈。

Box Junctions 黃格交界

Yellow criss-cross painted at junction:

  • Do NOT enter unless your exit is clear
  • Exception: when turning right, you may enter and wait if the only thing stopping you is oncoming traffic or other right-turning vehicles
  • Penalty for stopping in box junction: £70 (or more in London)

Level Crossings 鐵路平交道

  • NEVER stop on a level crossing
  • If lights flash red — STOP. If continue flashing after train passes, another is coming
  • If barriers come down while you're crossing: keep moving forward — don't reverse
  • If broken down on a crossing: get everyone out, push clear if possible, then use the railway phone
  • NEVER drive around lowered barriers
  • "Open" crossings (no barriers): look both ways, listen
平交道：紅燈閃必停；過程中千祈不要停；壞車要立即落車離開鐵軌，然後打鐵路專線電話。

Traffic Lights 交通燈

UK traffic light sequence:

  1. RED — STOP and wait behind the line
  2. RED + AMBER — STOP; prepare to go (do not start moving)
  3. GREEN — proceed only if your way is clear and you can do so safely
  4. AMBER — STOP unless you are so close that stopping might cause a collision
  5. Back to RED

Green arrow (filter): may go in direction of arrow only, even if main light is red.

Flashing amber (at pelican crossing): give way to pedestrians, proceed when clear.

Faulty / out of order: treat as unmarked junction — proceed with caution, no priority.

Overtaking 超車

  • Only overtake when it is safe and legal
  • Check mirrors, signal in good time
  • Allow plenty of room — especially for cyclists, motorcyclists, horses
  • Return to your lane only when you can see the overtaken vehicle in your interior mirror
  • Do not overtake on solid white lines on your side
  • Do not overtake near junctions, pedestrian crossings, bends, brows of hills
  • Do not exceed the speed limit to complete an overtake
  • Do not overtake on the left ("undertake"), except in heavy congested traffic or one-way streets

Lane Discipline 行車線紀律

  • Keep left — UK driving
  • Don't "hog" the middle lane on motorways/dual carriageways (offence: £100 + 3 points)
  • Choose the correct lane in good time at junctions and roundabouts
  • Don't change lanes at the last moment

Signals 訊號

  • Signal in good time — not too early (confusing), not too late (no warning)
  • Cancel signals after use
  • Hazard lights: only when stationary and warning of hazard, OR briefly on motorway/dual carriageway to warn of sudden congestion ahead
  • Brake lights = signal of slowing — touch the brake even if engine-braking to warn following traffic
  • Horn: only as a warning of your presence; NOT in built-up areas at night (23:30–07:00) except in emergency

One-Way Streets 單行路

  • Traffic flows in one direction only
  • Choose the correct lane as soon as you can
  • You may overtake on either side (left or right)
  • Watch for cyclists going the "wrong" way on contraflow cycle lanes

Priority Roads & Junctions 優先道路

  • "GIVE WAY" sign (inverted triangle): must give way to traffic on major road
  • "STOP" sign (octagon): must come to complete stop, then give way
  • Continuous white line across mouth of junction: traffic on your road has priority
  • Broken white line across mouth of junction: must give way
  • No road markings/signs: no priority — proceed with great caution
