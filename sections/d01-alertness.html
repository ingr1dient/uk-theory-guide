D1Alertness 專注力與警覺性

Sources: The Highway Code Rules 97–100, 148–150 · Road Traffic Act 1988 s.4 (driving whilst unfit) · Road Safety Act 2006 · DVSA Safe Driving for Life · DfT Think! campaign

Alertness means being fully attentive to the road, other users, and your own condition at all times. The theory test frequently asks about distractions, tiredness, and eyesight — often with very specific numbers.

Alertness係考試最常考嘅topic之一。考點集中喺：手機使用、疲勞駕駛嘅徵兆與處理、視力標準。

Concentration & Attention 專注力

  • Keep your eyes moving constantly — scan ahead, check mirrors every 5–8 seconds, check blind spots when changing direction
  • Look well ahead: at least 12 seconds at normal speeds to identify hazards early
  • Use the COAST method to avoid tunnel vision: Corners, Oncoming, Ahead, Sides, Trailing (behind)
  • Don't fix your eyes on one point — a fixed stare reduces peripheral awareness
眼睛要不停移動，唔好死盯住一個地方。每隔5–8秒望一次後視鏡。

Mobile Phones 手機使用

⚠ Law: Handheld Mobile Phone Ban (2022)
Using a handheld mobile phone whilst driving (or supervising a learner) is illegal — including when stationary in traffic. Penalty: £200 fine + 6 penalty points (new drivers lose licence immediately at 6 points).
  • Illegal (handheld): calling, texting, browsing, using sat-nav on phone held in hand
  • Legal (hands-free): phone mounted in cradle, using Bluetooth — but still a significant distraction
  • Even hands-free calls make you 4× more likely to crash — the cognitive distraction remains even without holding the device
  • You must pull over safely and park if you need to use your phone
  • Exception: calling 999 in a genuine emergency when it's not safe to stop
  • Since March 2022: the ban covers any use of a held phone — not just calls/texts
即使係停喺紅燈都唔可以用手持手機！即使hands-free都係危險嘅。2022年起，任何用途嘅手持手機都係違法。

Other Distractions 其他分心因素

  • Eating or drinking: takes one hand off wheel; can be prosecuted for not being in proper control
  • Loud music: masks external sounds (emergency sirens, horns, cyclists); impairs judgement
  • Passengers: engaging conversation can be as distracting as a phone call
  • In-car controls: adjust sat-nav, radio etc. only when safely stopped
  • External distractions: roadside accidents, billboards, attractive views — don't "rubber-neck"
  • Children/animals in the vehicle: address issues before driving or pull over safely

Tiredness & Fatigue 疲勞駕駛

⚠ Tiredness kills — no quick fix
Fatigue is a factor in up to 20% of road accidents in the UK and up to 25% of fatal crashes. The only cure is sleep.

High-risk times

  • 2am – 6am: deepest circadian trough (body clock at lowest)
  • 2pm – 4pm: post-lunch dip in alertness
  • Long motorway journeys: monotony accelerates fatigue

Warning signs of fatigue 疲勞警告徵兆

  • Yawning repeatedly
  • Heavy or closing eyelids
  • Difficulty concentrating; mind wandering
  • Drifting out of lane without noticing
  • Missing road signs or junctions
  • Losing memory of the last few miles driven
  • Head nodding forward
如果出現以上任何徵兆，要立即停車休息。唔好勉強繼續。

What to do 應對方法

  • Pull over safely — off the motorway at a service area or layby
  • Drink two cups of caffeinated coffee (or equivalent), then take a 15–20 minute nap — caffeine takes ~20 min to kick in; this is the most effective short-term strategy
  • Take a break of at least 15 minutes every 2 hours on long journeys
  • Plan journeys: avoid driving during high-risk hours if possible
  • Opening windows: only provides a few minutes' relief — not a solution
  • Turning up the radio: similarly ineffective long-term
  • Stopping on the motorway hard shoulder to sleep: illegal unless a genuine breakdown
最有效方法：飲完咖啡立即瞓20分鐘（caffeine nap）。開窗或大聲音樂係冇用嘅。

Eyesight Requirements 視力要求

20m
Plate reading distance (cars)
20/70
Snellen equiv. minimum
  • You must be able to read a standard number plate at 20 metres (about 5 car lengths) in good daylight — with glasses/contacts if worn
  • Glasses or contact lenses prescribed for driving must be worn every time you drive
  • Failing the eyesight test at your practical test = automatic failure; driving with inadequate eyesight = criminal offence
  • If eyesight deteriorates: stop driving; see an optician; inform DVLA if condition affects driving
  • Night driving: eyes need time to adapt from bright to dark — avoid looking directly at oncoming headlights; look to the left-hand side of the road
  • Keep windscreen clean inside and out — a dirty windscreen greatly worsens sun-glare visibility
眼力測試：喺20米外能夠讀出車牌（約5架車嘅距離）。如果需要眼鏡，每次揸車都要戴。

Medication & Medical Conditions 藥物與健康狀況

  • Some legal medications cause drowsiness: antihistamines, sleeping tablets, some antidepressants, strong painkillers
  • Check labels for "may cause drowsiness" — if present, do not drive
  • Ask your doctor or pharmacist whether medication affects driving ability
  • Driving while impaired by legal medication = same offence as drink-driving
  • You must notify the DVLA of certain medical conditions: epilepsy, sleep apnoea, diabetes (if on insulin), heart conditions, vision problems, dementia — failure to do so: fine up to £1,000
合法藥物都可以令你不適合駕駛！標籤上有「may cause drowsiness」就唔好揸車。某些健康問題必須通知DVLA。

Carbon Monoxide 一氧化碳

  • CO is odourless and colourless — you cannot detect it without a detector
  • Even low concentrations cause headache, dizziness, drowsiness, and impaired judgement
  • Never run the engine in an enclosed space (garage)
  • In heavy snow: check the exhaust pipe is not blocked before starting the engine
  • Fit a CO alarm in your garage if applicable
