D8Vehicle Handling 操車技術

Sources: The Highway Code Rules 117–122, 231–237 · The Official DVSA Guide to Driving – the essential skills Ch. 4–6 · Road Vehicles (Construction and Use) Regulations 1986

Vehicle handling covers how you physically control your car — steering, braking, gear changes, and responses to skids and adverse conditions. Good handling is smooth, predictable, and uses the vehicle's systems correctly.

Vehicle Handling係關於點樣實際控制車輛。考試重點：skid處理、ABS使用、coasting危險、engine braking。

Steering 轉向

  • Pull-push (feeding) steering: recommended for most driving — hands stay on the wheel, one pushes up while the other pulls down; avoids crossed arms and maintains control
  • Hand-over-hand (rotational): acceptable for tight manoeuvres such as parking or U-turns
  • Keep both hands on the wheel at all times unless using a vehicle control (gear lever, indicators etc.)
  • Don't rest your arm on the door while steering — reduces reaction time
  • Power steering failure: steering becomes much heavier but the vehicle is still steerable — slow down, pull over safely and get it checked
Pull-push steering係駕駛考試認可手法。揸車時唔好手放門邊靠住。

Skidding 車輛打滑

Key Principle
Most skids are caused by the driver — excessive speed, harsh steering, harsh braking, or harsh acceleration. The road surface, tyre condition, and load also contribute.

Types of skid & correction

Skid TypeWhat HappensCorrection廣東話
Rear-wheel skid (oversteer) Rear slides outward; car rotates Ease off accelerator; steer into the skid (same direction rear is sliding) 車尾甩尾，轉盤跟車尾方向
Front-wheel skid (understeer) Front tyres lose grip; car ploughs straight on Ease off accelerator; don't brake hard; wait for grip to return; straighten then steer 車頭唔跟轉盤，放油門等輪胎重新抓地
Four-wheel skid All wheels slide (usually from heavy braking) With ABS: maintain firm pressure. Without ABS: release and re-apply (cadence braking) ABS：持續踩。無ABS：間歇踩

ABS — Anti-lock Braking System 防鎖死剎車系統

  • ABS prevents wheel lock-up during emergency braking, allowing the driver to continue steering
  • With ABS: apply firm, continuous pressure to the brake pedal — do NOT pump the brakes
  • You will feel a pulsing/vibrating sensation through the pedal — this is normal, do not release
  • ABS does not shorten stopping distance on all surfaces — on gravel or loose surfaces it may increase it
  • ABS warning light on whilst driving = system fault — get it checked immediately
  • Mandatory on all new cars sold in the EU since 2004; effectively all modern cars have it
有ABS要持續踩剎車，唔好放開！感覺到腳板震動係正常嘅，代表ABS正在運作。

Coasting — Do Not Coast 滑行的危險

⚠ Coasting is dangerous
Coasting means travelling with the clutch pressed or gearbox in neutral. Always stay in gear.
  • Reduces engine braking: especially dangerous on downhill sections
  • Less control: rear-wheel drive cars lose steering feel when coasting
  • On hills: always select a lower gear before descending, rather than coasting down
  • Coming to a stop: it is acceptable to press the clutch just before stopping to prevent stalling — but avoid coasting distances at speed
Coasting（踩離合器滑行）係危險嘅！落斜時要用低波數引擎制動，唔好踩晒離合器落去。

Engine Braking & Braking on Hills 引擎制動與山坡行駛

  • Engine braking: selecting a lower gear on descents helps slow the car without using the footbrake — reduces risk of brake fade
  • Brake fade: brakes overheat from prolonged use on long descents → become less effective → dangerous. Prevent by using engine braking
  • Downhill: engage a lower gear before the descent begins, not after
  • Uphill starts: use handbrake (or hill-start assist if fitted) to prevent rolling back; coordinate clutch and accelerator
  • On a steep uphill where you may stall, be prepared to give way rather than cause an obstruction
落斜前要先換低波，唔好一直踩剎車（會令剎車過熱失效 = brake fade）。上斜用手剎配合，唔好向後溜。

Acceleration Sense 油門感

  • Acceleration sense: adjusting your speed by easing off the accelerator rather than braking — smoother, more fuel-efficient, safer
  • Don't accelerate in bends — can cause a skid; accelerate gently on the exit of a bend
  • Gentle, progressive acceleration reduces wear on tyres and drivetrain
  • In icy or wet conditions: very gentle acceleration to avoid wheelspin
  • Cruise control: only use on motorways/dual carriageways in clear conditions; switch off in bad weather, heavy traffic, or on winding roads

Gear Changes 換擋

  • Change up at ~2,000 rpm (petrol) or ~2,500 rpm (diesel) for fuel efficiency
  • Block changing (e.g. 5th → 3rd) is perfectly acceptable and common
  • Don't "ride" the clutch (keep it partially pressed) — causes premature clutch wear
  • Select the appropriate gear for your speed and road conditions before you need engine power
  • Automatic gearbox: D = Drive, R = Reverse, N = Neutral, P = Park; use Sport/manual mode for more engine braking on hills
「Block changing」係指跳波（例如直接由5波換3波），係完全合法合理嘅。

Aquaplaning 水上漂移

  • Occurs when a layer of water builds up between tyres and road — tyres lose contact with the tarmac
  • Steering feels unusually light; car no longer responds to steering inputs
  • Correction: ease off the accelerator gently; do NOT brake sharply; hold the steering wheel straight; wait for tyres to regain contact
  • Prevention: maintain correct tyre tread depth (≥1.6mm); reduce speed in heavy rain
水上漂（Aquaplaning）：方向盤突然變輕，車唔聽話。要輕輕放油門，唔好急剎！

Electronic Stability Control (ESC/ESP) 電子穩定控制

  • ESC detects and automatically corrects loss of traction/skidding by applying brakes to individual wheels
  • Mandatory on all new cars sold in the EU/UK since October 2014
  • Reduces rollover risk significantly — particularly important in SUVs and high-centre-of-gravity vehicles
  • ESC does not override the laws of physics — it reduces risk but cannot prevent all skids at excessive speed

Towing 拖行拖車

  • Increases overall length and weight → longer stopping distances
  • Affects steering, cornering, and reversing
  • Speed limits when towing: built-up areas 30mph; single carriageway 50mph; dual carriageway 60mph; motorway 60mph
  • Extended door mirrors required if the trailer is wider than the towing vehicle's mirrors
  • Snaking/fishtailing (trailer swinging side to side): ease off accelerator smoothly; do NOT brake suddenly; allow speed to reduce naturally until the trailer stabilises
  • Maximum trailer width: 2.55 metres
  • Check nose weight and total train weight against manufacturer's limits
拖車時速度限制較低（單車路50mph，雙線快速路及高速公路60mph）。如果拖車左右擺動，輕放油門，唔好急剎！
