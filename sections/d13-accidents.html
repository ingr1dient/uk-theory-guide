D13Accidents & Emergencies 意外與緊急情況

Sources: The Highway Code Rules 281–287 · Road Traffic Act 1988 s.170 (duty to stop & report) · Resuscitation Council UK Guidelines 2021 (CPR) · Annex 7 of The Highway Code (First Aid)

At the Scene of an Accident 事故現場處理

  1. STOP — you are legally required to stop if involved
  2. Warn other traffic: switch on hazard lights, use warning triangle (if not on motorway — 45m behind)
  3. Call 999 if anyone is injured, the road is blocked, or there is danger
  4. Assess injuries using DRABC (see below)
  5. Do NOT move injured people unless in immediate danger (e.g., fire)
  6. Do NOT remove a motorcyclist's helmet unless trained — risks worsening spinal injury
  7. Switch off ignitions of involved vehicles to reduce fire risk
  8. Keep bystanders away, do not allow smoking
  9. Exchange details with other drivers: name, address, vehicle reg, insurance, phone
  10. If you can't exchange details: report to police within 24 hours
到現場：先STOP停車 → 開警示燈警告其他車 → 有傷者或危險就打999 → 用DRABC評估 → 唔好郁傷者（除非有即時危險如火警）→ 唔好除電單車頭盔。

DRABC — Emergency Assessment DRABC 急救評估

📋 DRABC
Danger · Response · Airway · Breathing · Circulation
  • Danger: check the area is safe — for you and the casualty
  • Response: shake shoulders, ask "Are you okay?" Check for any response
  • Airway: tilt head back gently to open airway, check for obstructions
  • Breathing: look, listen, feel for breath (max 10 seconds)
  • Circulation: check for signs of life; if no breathing, begin CPR

Basic First Aid 基本急救

  • Recovery position: for unconscious but breathing casualties — on their side, head tilted, top leg bent
  • CPR ratio: 30 chest compressions : 2 rescue breaths, push hard and fast (about 100–120 per minute)
  • Bleeding: apply firm pressure with clean material; elevate if possible; do not remove embedded objects
  • Shock: keep warm, lie down, raise legs if no leg injury, reassure, do not give food/drink
  • Burns: cool with cold water at least 10 minutes; do not remove stuck clothing
CPR比例：30下按壓 : 2次人工呼吸。胸口按壓要快要深（每分鐘100–120次）。

Reporting Accidents 事故報告

You must inform the police as soon as possible (within 24 hours) if:

  • Anyone is injured
  • You cannot exchange details with other parties
  • Damage caused to property (including animals — except cats)
  • The accident involves certain animals: horse, cow, ass/mule, sheep, pig, goat, dog

Within 7 days, you must also produce: driving licence, insurance certificate, MOT (if required) at a police station.

報警：有傷者或換唔到資料，要24小時內報警；之後7日內去警署出示駕照、保險、MOT。（撞到貓唔使報，其他如馬、牛、狗要報）

Vehicle Fire 車輛火警

  1. Stop as safely and quickly as possible
  2. Switch off the engine
  3. Get everyone OUT, well away from the vehicle
  4. Call 999
  5. Do NOT return to the vehicle — fuel tank may explode

Tunnel Emergencies 隧道緊急情況

  • Switch on dipped headlights when entering tunnels (even in daytime)
  • Keep distance from vehicle ahead
  • Listen to radio if frequency given on tunnel signs
  • If broken down: switch on hazards, use emergency phone in tunnel
  • If fire: drive out if possible. If not, leave keys in ignition, exit on foot, use emergency exits

Level Crossing Emergency 平交道緊急

  1. Get everyone out of the vehicle immediately
  2. Push the vehicle clear if possible and safe
  3. Use the railway phone (on or near the crossing) to inform the signaller
  4. If train is coming and you can't move the vehicle — leave it, move clear

Breakdowns (Non-Motorway) 非高速公路壞車

  • Get vehicle off the road if possible (lay-by, side street)
  • Switch on hazard lights
  • If on the carriageway, place a warning triangle 45 metres (50 yards) behind — but NOT on motorways
  • Wear high-visibility clothing
  • Keep passengers safe — away from passing traffic
  • Call breakdown service
